How We Work

How long does a website take?

Most websites are delivered in 2 weeks from start to finish. Here's the typical timeline:

  • Week 1: Discovery, design, and content
  • Week 2: Development, testing, and launch

More complex sites may take longer ‚Äî we'll give you a clear timeline upfront.

Do you use templates?

No. Every website we build is custom-designed and coded from scratch. Templates are slow, bloated, and bad for SEO. We write clean, fast code that's optimized for your business.

What if I don't like the design?

We work closely with you during the design phase to make sure you love it before we build. We include 2 rounds of revisions in our standard package. Our goal is your complete satisfaction.

Will I be able to update the site myself?

Yes. We build sites that are easy to update. For simple changes (text, photos), you can do it yourself. For more complex updates, we're always here to help ‚Äî or we can show you how.

Do you write the content?

We can, but we prefer you do. You know your business best. We provide guidance on what to write and optimize it for SEO. If you need help, we can recommend copywriters or write basic content for an additional fee.

About SEO

How long does SEO take to work?

Most clients see initial improvements within 2-4 weeks, with significant results in 6-8 weeks. SEO is a long game ‚Äî the work we do now compounds over time. Our case study with M&K Painting saw them hit #3 for their target keyword in 8 weeks.

Do you guarantee rankings?

No one can guarantee #1 rankings ‚Äî and you should be suspicious of anyone who does. What we guarantee is that we'll follow best practices, do the work, and give you transparent reporting. Most of our clients see significant ranking improvements.

What areas do you serve?

We're based in Adelaide and specialize in the northern suburbs: Mawson Lakes, Golden Grove, Gawler, Modbury, Para Hills, and surrounding areas. That said, we work with clients across all of South Australia.

You can, but results are limited. If your current site is slow, not mobile-friendly, or poorly structured, you're fighting an uphill battle. We often recommend starting with a solid foundation (new site) and then layering SEO on top.
